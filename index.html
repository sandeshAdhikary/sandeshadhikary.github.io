About Me

I'm a PhD candidate at the Paul G. Allen School of Computer Science at the University of Washington , advised by Professor Byron Boots.

My research has been focused on quantum-inspired probabilistic models for sequential data. In particular, Iâ€™ve worked on understanding how these models relate to models from classical machine learning in terms of expressiveness and learnability. Currently, I am working on applying some of these models from stochastic processes and tensor networks to the problem of learning parameters of quantum systems from experimental data. I am also broadly interested in identifying and exploiting geometric structure underlying various problems in machine learning. Recently, I have been working on kernel-based sampling methods applied to problems that require sampling over non-Euclidean Riemannian manifolds.
